Common Heart Health Questions

What are the early warning signs of a heart attack? expand_more
Early warning signs can include chest discomfort (pressure, squeezing, or fullness), shortness of breath, pain in one or both arms, the back, neck, jaw or stomach, and breaking out in a cold sweat. It's crucial to seek emergency medical attention if these symptoms persist for more than a few minutes.
How often should I check my heart health? expand_more
For most adults over 20, a heart health screening including blood pressure, cholesterol, and BMI should occur every 4-6 years. However, if you have high risk factors, annual check-ups are highly recommended by the American Heart Association.
Can stress directly damage my heart tissue? expand_more
Chronic stress increases the levels of cortisol and adrenaline in the body, which can lead to increased heart rate and blood pressure, potentially damaging arterial linings over time. Managing stress is a critical component of cardiac health management.
